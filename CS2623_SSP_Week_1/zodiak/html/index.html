Year of the Rat

The Year of the Rat ranks first in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. People born in the Year of the Rat are known to be instinctive, acute and alert. They can always react properly before the worst circumstances take place. They are also sophisticated and popular when in social interactions. People born in the Year Of The Rat are typically very otpimistic and/or positive even when faced with overwhelming odds against them.

Year of the Ox

The Year of the Ox ranks second in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. People often use it to indicate something big in size or number. People born in the Year of Ox are persistent, simple, honest, and straightforward. They are talent leaders with strong devotion to thier work. They are also contemplative before taking immediate actions are not and easily affected by the surroundings. These people also often default to the old habits.

Year of the Tiger

The Year of the Tiger ranks third in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. In most cases, people with Chinese zodiac sign of the Tiger are very powerful, independent, confident and brave people. They are frank and easy to win others' trust. In their middle age, their fate may be uneven, but after hardships, they will enjoy a bright prospect. While they are also likely to be dogmatic, and like showing off when accomplishing something.

Year of the Rabbit

The Year of the Rabbit ranks fourth in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. People with the Chinese Zodiac Rabbit sign impress others with thier affectionate tenderness, grace and sensitivity. They are also romantic in their relationships and they also having a have a high demand for their quality of life. They avoid arguing with others if at all possible and have a capability of converting an enemy into a friend.

Year of the Dragon

The Year of the Dragon ranks fifth in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. People with Chinese zodiac sign Dragon are typically lively, intellectual and excitable. They can clearly tell right from wrong and are upright and frank, however, they are also a bit arrogant and impatient. In contrast to male Dragons, female Dragons tend to be overly confident. They are not afraid of difficulties and they hate to be used or controlled by others.

Year of the Sanke

The Year of the Snake ranks sixth in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. People usually regard the snake as a sly and cunning animal, which likes hanging out in darkness. The Snake is a symbol of wit and wisdom, and people with Chinese Zodiac sign of Snake are also sensitive and humorous. Most people that are Snake are gifted in literature and art, such as Goethe and Picasso. People with the Chinese Zodiak sign Sanke are always a bit suspicious, which is what makes them hesitant and a bit paranoid.

Year of the Horse

The Year of the Horse ranks seventh in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. They tend to impress upon people with thier dynamic, zealous and generous image. Although endowed with many shinning points, they have to face the weaknesses in their characteristics such as a lack of persistnace, generously spending and frank attitude.

Year of the Goat

The Year of the Goat ranks eigth in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. People born in the Year of Goat are tender, polite, filial, clever, and kind-hearted. They have fondness for quiet living. They are also wise, gentle and compassionate. In their daily life, they try to be economical and they are willing to take care of others. They should also avoid pessimism and hesitation.

Year of the Monkey

The Year of the Monkey ranks ninth in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. The general image of people born in the Year of the Monkey is of always being smart, clever and intelligent. They are also very lively, flexible, quick-witted. They have several shortcomings, such as a temper and a tendency to look down upon others.

Year of the Rooster

The Year of the Rooster ranks tenth in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. These people have many excellent characteristics, such as being honest, bright, communicative and ambitious. Most are born with a very polished look and they prefer to dress up. In their daily lives they tend not to rely on others. Their strengths are being independent, capable and warm hearted.

Year of the Dog

The Year of the Dog ranks eleventh in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. People born in the Year of the Dog are usually independent, sincere, loyal and decisive. They are not afraid of difficulties of daily life. These characteristics make them have harmonious relationships with the people around them.

Year of the Pig

The Year of the Pig ranks twelth in the years of the Chinese Zodiak. People with Chinese zodiac Pig are often considerd, responsible, independent and optimistic. They are generous and have very good interpersonal relationships with the people around them. However, they are sometimes lazy and lack motivation to take action. Their pure hearts let them be cheated easily in daily life.
